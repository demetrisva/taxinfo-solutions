Residency and Filing Scope

The 2026 individual tax changes matter most when taxpayers understand which Cyprus residence and filing rules actually apply to them.

183-Day Rule

Residence can still arise by spending more than 183 days in Cyprus during the calendar year.

✈️

60-Day Rule

The Cyprus 60-day rule remains important for mobile professionals who are not tax resident elsewhere and maintain a qualifying home and business or employment tie in Cyprus.

📝

Wider Filing Duty

From 2026, the reform package broadens annual return filing to Cyprus tax residents aged 18 to 65, even where income is nil, subject to the final detailed guidance and exclusions.

Key Cyprus Tax Rules to Know

Short-form explainers on the regimes readers ask about most often.

🌍 Non-Dom Position Legacy advantage

The tax incentives material continues to highlight the appeal of Cyprus for non-domiciled individuals, especially around SDC exposure on dividends, interest and rents. The exact benefit still depends on personal residence, domicile history and the latest law wording.

🚀 First Employment Relief 50% exemption

The incentives package continues to support a 50% first-employment exemption where statutory conditions are met, including salary thresholds and residence criteria. High earners relocating to Cyprus should document eligibility early.

🏖️ Foreign Pension Option 5% route

Cyprus continues to offer a favourable option for foreign pension income, including a 5% tax route on annual foreign pension amounts above EUR 3,420. The choice should be reviewed alongside the standard progressive scale.

🌱 Green and Digital Measures 2026 changes

The 2026 package combines a new 8% crypto gains rule with deduction support for qualifying green spending. Businesses should also monitor whether the zero-rate VAT relief on basic goods affects their invoicing and product setup through December 31, 2026.
