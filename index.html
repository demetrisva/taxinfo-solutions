2026 Summary

Key Cyprus Tax Changes for 2026

The 2026 filing year brings simultaneous changes across personal, corporate, VAT and filing scope rules. Here is a plain summary of the most important shifts.

Personal Income Tax

The tax-free threshold increased from EUR 19,500 to EUR 22,000 effective January 1, 2026. The progressive bands above the threshold were also restructured: 20% applies on income from EUR 22,001 to EUR 32,000, 25% on EUR 32,001 to EUR 42,000, 30% on EUR 42,001 to EUR 72,000, and 35% on income above EUR 72,000.

For most employees, the higher threshold means a reduction in annual income tax compared to 2025. The size of the saving depends on gross salary, applicable deductions, and social contribution levels. Use the calculator on the Tools page for a directional estimate specific to your income level.

Corporate Tax and Dividend SDC

The Cyprus corporate income tax rate increased to 15% from January 1, 2026, up from 12.5%. This change affects all Cyprus tax-resident companies and has knock-on effects for deferred tax positions, provisional tax estimates, and board-level distribution planning. Finance teams should update their 2026 forecasts to use the new rate and review deferred tax assets and liabilities on the balance sheet.

Separately, the Special Defence Contribution rate on dividends was reduced to 5% for Cyprus-domiciled resident shareholders. Combined with the 15% corporate tax rate, the effective combined rate on profits distributed as dividends is approximately 19.25% for Cyprus-resident domiciled shareholders.

Filing Scope: Who Must File

Official business guidance for 2026 indicates that Cyprus tax residents aged between 25 and 71 are expected to file an annual tax return, regardless of income level. This is broader than the filing scope that many individuals understood to apply in prior years. Residency is established through either the 183-day physical presence test or the 60-day qualifying test, both of which require contemporaneous documentation to support.

Individuals who have been relying on their employer's PAYE withholding as a substitute for personal filing should review this position. Withholding removes the cash tax liability but does not substitute for the annual return filing obligation for those within the filing scope.

VAT: Zero Rate and SME Scheme

A temporary zero VAT rate applies to qualifying basic goods through December 31, 2026. Businesses selling basic goods must ensure their invoicing systems correctly classify zero-rated supplies and that product evidence is on file to support the treatment. The zero rate reverts to the standard 19% rate from January 1, 2027, so year-end controls and pricing updates are required before December 31.

A separate small-enterprise VAT exemption scheme, introduced from January 1, 2025, exempts businesses below a turnover threshold from VAT registration obligations. Businesses near the threshold should monitor their position and confirm their scheme status for 2026 and beyond.
