2026 Summary

Key Cyprus tax changes for 2026

The 2026 filing year brings simultaneous changes across personal, corporate, VAT and filing-scope rules. This summary keeps the major shifts visible in one place for accountants and business readers.

Personal income tax

The tax-free threshold increased from EUR 19,500 to EUR 22,000 from January 1, 2026. The progressive bands above the threshold also moved, which is why many salary comparisons in 2026 need a full annual view rather than a quick monthly assumption.

For many employees, the higher threshold reduces annual income tax compared with 2025, but the practical impact still depends on gross income, social contributions, and deduction support. The calculator workspace is best used as a directional estimate before relying on the long-form salary briefing.

Corporate tax and dividend SDC

The Cyprus corporate income tax rate increased to 15% from January 1, 2026. That affects forecasts, deferred tax balances, provisional tax estimates, and year-end planning across Cyprus tax-resident companies.

The Special Defence Contribution rate on dividends was reduced to 5% for Cyprus-domiciled resident shareholders. In practice, boards and finance teams should read the company-rate and shareholder-rate changes together rather than treating them as separate planning questions.

Filing scope and residency

Official business guidance for 2026 indicates broader filing scope, including Cyprus tax residents aged 25 to 71 regardless of income, subject to implementation detail. That makes the filing question almost as important as the rate question for many individuals.

Residency still depends on the 183-day or 60-day framework, but documentary support matters more when filing scope widens. Readers using PAYE-only assumptions should check whether withholding solves the cash point but not the return-filing obligation.

VAT zero rate and SME scheme

A temporary zero VAT rate applies to qualifying basic goods through December 31, 2026. Businesses using the relief need product-level support, invoice consistency, and a dated transition plan before the rate reverts.

The small-enterprise VAT exemption scheme introduced from January 1, 2025 remains relevant for 2026. Businesses near the threshold should recheck their position before they assume VAT treatment is stable for the full year.
