Qualified Stock Options at 8%: Approval Windows and Controls

A founder-facing note on the approved employer incentive scheme rules and the internal documentation startups should not leave until a financing round.

Back to Briefing Hub
Startup briefing 3 sections 1 official source

The incentives package created a real operational question for startups: whether the scheme is approved, documented and explained well enough for the tax treatment to hold.

What the incentives package introduces

Rule summary

March 10, 2026

The Ministry of Finance incentives package introduces an 8% tax on gains from the disposal of shares issued under approved employer incentive schemes. It also states that plans already granted before January 1, 2026 may still qualify if approval is obtained by June 30, 2026.

That June 30 window is the operational pressure point for companies with legacy plans or informal founder-era arrangements.

  • New approved schemes can access the 8% treatment.
  • Older plans may still qualify if approved by June 30, 2026.
  • Approval status should be documented early, not after exit discussions begin.

What startups should put on file

Practical application

March 10, 2026

Keep the scheme rules, approval evidence, board resolutions, grant notices, vesting schedule and cap table in one controlled location. If employees are expected to rely on tax-efficient treatment, the company should be able to show the scheme was designed and approved correctly.

Investor updates should also be consistent with the actual plan terms. A tax incentive becomes much harder to defend when the legal documents and the pitch deck tell different stories.

  • Store grant notices with the approved scheme documents.
  • Reconcile vesting schedules to the cap table regularly.
  • Align employee communications with the actual scheme terms.

What founders should not overstate

Boundary note

March 10, 2026

The 8% treatment is attractive, but it does not remove the need for proper approvals, legal drafting and payroll or reporting analysis. Founders should avoid presenting the relief as automatic until the scheme status is confirmed.

Use this page as a process checklist and then validate the company-specific facts with legal and tax advisers.

Startup checklist

  • Confirm whether the scheme is approved or needs approval.
  • Check the June 30, 2026 window for pre-2026 grants.
  • Keep board resolutions, grants and vesting files together.
  • Align employee, investor and legal documents.
